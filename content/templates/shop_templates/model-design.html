Master Signature:   Dale Wilson

Dale Wilson joined Fender in 2003, joined Fender Custom Stores in 2005, and became Master Builder in 2011. A Native of California, he had a passion for making guitars from an early age. "For as long as I can remember, I've loved guitars," he says. "Strangely enough, I didn't want to be a rock star. I wanted to make a super guitar. The Wilson family is proficient in carpentry, and exquisite craftsmanship has always been the passion and pursuit of Fender custom shop, which has long been integrated into dell's blood. Back in the late 1980s, he was working in guitar repair and tooling at a studio near his middle school.

He then went to work for Dobro and Rickenbacker for a while before entering Fender; He also worked for guitar brands such as Guild, Gretsch and Benedetto before becoming an apprentice to a master luthier in a custom shop. As a craftsman, he relished being able to draw on his extensive experience in producing high-quality guitars, such as the Resophonic Thinline Telecaster. In 2011, while still an apprentice, he made five premium custom guitars for the NAMM Show in January of that year and was asked to sign the back, earning him the honor of Master Builder.
